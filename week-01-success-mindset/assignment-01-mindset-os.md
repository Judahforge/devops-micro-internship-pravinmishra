# Assignment 1. What is something you believe to be true that most people around you would disagree with?

### Rules

* No "safe" answers.
* Must be your real belief (not copied from internet).
* Minimum 50 words.

**Hint:** What do you believe about career, money, learning, discipline, relationships, health, success, life, tech industry, etc. that most people don't agree with?

## Answer

One belief I have that many people around me would probably disagree with is that **everything in life is about time**. No matter what your current situation looks like, enjoy the moment because it will not stay the same forever. Whether life is good or bad, time changes everything.

Some people get offended whenever I tell them this because they believe their current situation is permanent. I don't see it that way. I believe that if life feels like it is turning against you, it is only a season. Likewise, if everything is going well, that season will not last forever either. Time has a way of changing every situation.

When I look back at my own life, I remember moments when I felt left behind. There were experiences I thought I should have had, but I didn't because my life was different from that of my peers. At the time, it felt painful and unfair. Looking back now, I smile because those moments passed, and I became stronger because of them. If I had judged my life only by those difficult moments, I would have been wrong.

There are also times when I work very hard, yet it seems like I have nothing to show for my effort. It is easy for people to think they are wasting their energy or that they are not making progress. However, I have learned that time eventually reveals the value of consistent effort. It shows whether you have truly been lazy or whether your hard work simply needed more time to produce results.

For me, time is the greatest test. It reveals a person's character, proves whether a decision was wise or foolish, and eventually exposes what is real. Our emotions, our circumstances, our successes, and our failures are all temporary. That is why I believe everything in life is about time. No matter what season you are in today, time will eventually tell the full story.

# Assignment 2. What are the top 3 objective truths you discovered through experimentation and results?

### Definition

Objective truths do not depend on opinions. They hold true regardless of how people feel.

Write each truth in this format:

**Truth:** (1 sentence)

**Evidence from my life:** (2–4 lines: what you tried + what happened)

---

## Truth #1

### Truth

Shortcuts often lead to bigger problems in the long run.

### Evidence from my life

I learned this while taking a software engineering course. Whenever I got stuck on assignments, I copied my friends' work instead of taking the time to understand it. At first, it saved me time, but I later realized those assignments were the foundation for more advanced topics. Because I had skipped the learning process, I eventually fell behind, could not keep up with the course, and was unable to complete the program. Since then, I have learned that following the process is always better than looking for shortcuts.
---

## Truth #2

### Truth

There is never a perfect time to start.

### Evidence from my life

After secondary school, I delayed going to university for two years because I kept waiting for the "perfect time." I thought I needed more money and better circumstances before taking the next step. Eventually, I decided to start with what I had and trust that I would figure things out along the way. Once I made that decision, opportunities and resources began to fall into place. That experience taught me that action creates clarity, while waiting for perfect conditions only wastes time.

---

## Truth #3

### Truth

You cannot fool yourself, even if you fool other people.

### Evidence from my life

I realized this through my academic experience. Whenever I performed poorly in some courses, I blamed the lecturer or the marking system when people asked about my results. However, deep down, I knew the real reason was that I had not prepared as well as I should have. I could convince other people with excuses, but I could never hide the truth from myself. That experience taught me that honest self-reflection is the first step toward improvement.
